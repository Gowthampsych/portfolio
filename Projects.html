Disease Prediction using Machine Learning

    Disease Prediction using Machine Learning is a project where I developed a predictive model using decision tree, random forest, and naive Bayes algorithms to accurately predict diseases based on symptoms provided by the user. The model was trained on a dataset of known symptoms and diseases, allowing it to make accurate predictions. By entering symptoms into the system, users can receive predictions for which disease they may be suffering from, providing a potentially life-saving tool for early diagnosis and treatment.

MULTIPLE PEOPLE AND OBJECT TRACKING SYSTEM

A reliable and effective system for tracking and recognising many things and people in real-time is what the Multiple People and Object Tracking System aims to achieve. In order to accurately recognise and track objects and people, as well as to handle occlusions and track many objects at once, the will make use of computer vision and deep learning techniques. In order to improve situational awareness and offer useful insights for security and surveillance applications, the system will be built to integrate with current surveillance and security systems. YOLOv7 and other deep learning-based algorithms will be used by the system to detect and track people and objects in real time. To manage occlusions and monitor numerous objects simultaneously, these methods will be improved.

LANS-Large-scale-Arabic-News-Summarization-Corpus

Text summarization has been intensively studied in many languages, and some languages have reached advanced stages. Yet, Arabic Text Summarization (ATS) is still in its developing stages. Existing ATS datasets are either small or lack diversity. We build, LANS, a large-scale and diverse dataset for Arabic Text Summarization task. LANS offers 8.4 million articles and their summaries extracted from newspapers websitesâ€™ metadata between 1999 and 2019. The high-quality and diverse summaries are written by journalists from 22 major Arab newspapers, and include an eclectic mix of at least more than 7 topics from each source. We conduct an intrinsic evaluation on LANS by both automatic and human evaluations. Human evaluation of 1000 random samples reports 95.4% accuracy for our collected summaries, and automatic evaluation quantifies the diversity and abstractness of the summaries.
